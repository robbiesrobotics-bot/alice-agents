## Slide 1: The Problem

**Headline:** Building an AI-native team today is expensive, slow, and broken.

**Talking points:**
- The average developer or startup spends **3-6 months** integrating AI into their workflows — stitching together APIs, prompts, agents, and memory from scratch.
- Enterprise AI teams cost **$300K–$1M+/year** in tooling, infrastructure, and engineer time.
- Off-the-shelf AI tools are single-purpose: you get a coding assistant *or* a writing tool *or* a research bot — never a coordinated team.
- Prompt drift, context loss, and inconsistent output quality destroy trust in AI systems at scale.
- The promise of "AI coworkers" remains mostly vaporware — demos without real operational depth.

**What to say:** "Everyone's told that AI will change how we work. But right now, getting there costs a small fortune and months of engineering. We're here to collapse that timeline to a single command."

## Slide 2: The Solution

**Headline:** One command. An instant AI organization.

**Talking points:**
- `npx @robbiesrobotics/alice-agents` — that's it. In under 60 seconds, you have a 28-agent AI team ready to work.
- A.L.I.C.E. (Automated Layered Intelligence Coordination Engine) is a **pre-wired AI org chart**: each agent has a defined role, domain expertise, and communication protocol.
- Agents collaborate through Olivia (the orchestrator) — just like a real team where a manager delegates to specialists.
- No account required to start. No API setup maze. No custom prompt engineering.
- Built on battle-tested infrastructure: Claude Sonnet 4.5, persistent memory, multi-channel (Telegram, iMessage, CLI).

**What to say:** "We didn't just make AI easier — we made it *organizational*. The structure that took your team months to build, we ship it as a default."

## Slide 3: Product Demo Narrative

**Headline:** From zero to AI team in 60 seconds.

**Walk through this narrative:**

1. **Install** — Open terminal. Run: `npx @robbiesrobotics/alice-agents`
   - A beautiful CLI installer boots up. Agent roster loads. Olivia greets you.
   - First message: *"Hi, I'm Olivia. I coordinate your A.L.I.C.E. team. What are we working on today?"*

2. **First conversation** — Type: *"I need a product launch plan for my new SaaS tool."*
   - Olivia parses intent, identifies the task type, and routes to relevant specialists.
   - Morgan (Marketing) drafts the positioning. Clara (Communication) structures the deliverable. Rex (Research) pulls competitive context.

3. **Real output** — Within seconds:
   - A structured launch plan lands in your terminal (or Telegram, or email).
   - Formatted, specific, actionable — not a hallucinated blob of text.
   - Olivia asks: *"Want me to have Selena check this for legal risk, or should we move to execution?"*

4. **The moment of magic** — The user realizes they just got the output of a 3-person strategy session in under 2 minutes.

**What to say:** "This isn't a chatbot. It's a team. And it behaves like one."
