### 2026-03-21 — Built executive scorecard for A.L.I.C.E. ecosystem after rapid shipping window
- **Outcome:** success
- **What worked:** Anchoring the scorecard in shipped evidence (repo state, release notes, live surfaces, test results) prevented over-crediting polished marketing. Separating "real vs externally provable" was the sharpest executive lens.
- **What to improve:** For ecosystem reviews, gather authenticated screenshots or demo access earlier; public fetch alone underrepresents private operational tools.
- **Reusable pattern:** For founder-led product audits, score each surface on two axes: operational reality and proof visibility. A product can be real internally while still weak commercially if outsiders cannot verify it.

### 2026-03-17 — Created A.L.I.C.E. self-sustaining business project plan
- **Outcome:** success
- **What worked:** Breaking the plan into Rob-decision-required vs team-autonomous clearly surfaced the critical path. Defining the cron schedule table and agent ownership map made the plan concrete vs aspirational.
- **What to improve:** Future project plans should include a risk register from the start (added here but could be more detailed).
- **Reusable pattern:** For agent-driven automation projects: always map triggers (cron, webhook, event) alongside the agent task. "Morgan runs marketing" is vague; "Morgan runs Monday 8pm cron → blog post published Tuesday" is actionable. Decision queues with priority ratings (P0/P1/P2) are effective for surfacing Rob's most blocking items.
