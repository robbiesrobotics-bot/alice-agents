### 2026-03-21 — A.L.I.C.E. ecosystem messaging audit across landing, cloud, admin, and README
- **Outcome:** success
- **What worked:** Auditing all surfaces together instead of individually exposed the real issue: not brand inconsistency alone, but product-ladder ambiguity between local, Pro, self-hosted Mission Control, and Mission Control Cloud. Screenshots were especially useful for judging whether the admin console felt operational or merely decorative.
- **What to improve:** Could have gone one level deeper on Mission Control with authenticated access or source inspection; the public login page limited assessment of post-login promise delivery.
- **Reusable pattern:** For product credibility audits, compare four layers in order: promise (landing), proof (README/docs), delivery surface (customer app), and operator surface (internal/admin). Credibility usually breaks at the handoff between promise and delivery, not in the hero copy itself.

### 2026-03-17 — A.L.I.C.E. pricing strategy rethink (subscription vs one-time)
- **Outcome:** success
- **What worked:** Direct honest product analysis first (is this SaaS or software?), then structured options with clear pros/cons, strong recommendation with migration path
- **What to improve:** Could have done competitive pricing research via web_search for more precise comps — went with known benchmarks instead
- **Reusable pattern:** When pricing doesn't match product delivery model, lead with honesty assessment before options. Developer tools specifically: one-time purchase is the trust signal, cloud services are the revenue layer. "Land with one-time, expand with cloud" is a defensible investor narrative.
