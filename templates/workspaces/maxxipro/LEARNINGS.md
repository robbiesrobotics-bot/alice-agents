# LEARNINGS.md - MaxxiPro's Lesson Log

## Roof Maxx Insights

### 2026-04-10: The insurance angle is the differentiator
- Property managers and insurance carriers are the highest-value B2B targets
- The flex test + Roof Inspection Report is the key sales tool for insurance conversations
- Jesse should lead with the guarantee and the documented proof of shingle viability

### 2026-04-10: Pricing context
- Default rate: $2.50/sq ft (confirm for Northern Virginia market)
- Treatment cost = 25-35% of full replacement
- Upsell: Roof Tune-Up is included but should be emphasized

### 2026-04-10: What doesn't work
- HomeAdvisor/lead aggregators — expensive, not effective
- Google AdWords — nobody has succeeded with it for Roof Maxx
- Broad residential direct mail — not cost-effective

### 2026-04-10: What works
- LinkedIn outreach with Roof Maxx videos (stream in-feed)
- Direct mail (corporate-assisted — Roof Maxx sends on dealer's behalf)
- REIA clubs and real estate investor groups
- CAI (Community Associations Institute) local chapters
- Chamber of Commerce relationships
- "Treat one, get a neighborhood" play with HOA presidents

## B2B Outreach Patterns

### Property Manager outreach
- Lead with no-disruption and OPEX vs CAPEX framing
- Offer free inspection of one property as proof of concept

### Insurance adjuster outreach
- Lead with the contested repair-vs-replace problem
- Offer the Florida case study as proof

### HOA/condo board outreach
- Lead with the budget problem (special assessments)
- Offer free board president roof inspection

## Mistakes to Avoid

1. Don't use made-up stats — only Roof Maxx corporate data
2. Don't be pushy — Roof Maxx sells itself when explained correctly
3. Don't skip the flex test — it's the qualifying proof point
4. Don't forget to register the warranty after each job
